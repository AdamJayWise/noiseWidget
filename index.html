Signal to Noise - The Ideal and the Practical

Cryptic plots showing "Signal to Noise Ratio" vs "Photon Counts" are often used to present scientific camera performance.

  • Where do these numbers come from?
  • How can we interpret them?
  • What bearing do they have on image quality?
  • How can we measure them in the real world?

Below - an interactive signal-to-noise curve, with corresponding simulated images and line profiles. This curve represents the ideal relationship between photon count and signal-to-noise ratio, given the quantum efficiency and read noise indicated. In this contrived situation, a perfectly circular mask is used to project uniform illumination onto the middle of a camera sensor. For each frame, the ratio of signal (mean value of the pixels in the illuminated circle) to noise (standard deviation of the same) is calculated and indicated on the y axis. On the line profile, the mean +- one standard deviation is indicated.
